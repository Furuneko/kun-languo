{% extends "global/Page.html" %} {% load otree static %} {% block title %} Role {% endblock %} {% block content %} Screen 12: Uncontrollable Events [All roles and all conditions] Uncontrollable Event to Output Much like in the real world, sometimes employees’ output could be affected by an “uncontrollable event.” An example would be car salespeople’s performance would be affected by extreme weather conditions or the unexpected opening of a competing dealership. In this study, uncontrollable events may affect employees by increasing or decreasing their production of completed decodes. The event’s effect can go both directions and ranges between -5 and +5 decodes. For example, if an employee completed 25 decodes and was affected by a positive uncontrollable event of +5, she would have a realized output of 30 decodes. If she was affected by a negative uncontrollable event of -3, then her realized output would be 22 decodes.
